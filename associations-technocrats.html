Decennial Edition of National Level IT Fest Joshiana

more images

The Department of Computer Applications of St Joseph Engineering College (SJEC), Mangaluru, hosted 10th edition of its Mega Event Joshiana, a 2-day National Level IT Fest on 26th and 27th September 2019.

Joshiana 10.0 was inaugurated in the presence of dignitaries which included Mr Shripada Hebbar, Principal Technical Architect at Robosoft Technologies, Udupi, who was the Chief Guest at the Inaugural. The other dignitaries present on the dais were Rev. Fr Wilfred Prakash D’Souza, Director of SJEC who presided over the program, Rev. Fr Rohith D’Costa and Rev. Fr Alwyn Richard D’Souza Assistant Directors of SJEC, Dr Rio D’Souza, Principal of SJEC, Ms Sumangala N, HOD of MCA at SJEC, Mr Gururaja S, Convener of Joshiana 10.0, Mr Prajwal B, President of TechnoCrats and Ms Aishwarya-Secretary of TechnoCrats.

Mr Prajwal B briefed the gathering of the events that would be held over the 2 days. This was followed by the unveiling of the trophies for which the participants were competing against each other.

Dr Rio D’Souza spoke about the importance of the journey from being performers to reformers and to go on to become transformers. He mentioned the exemplary Josh shown the staff of the MCA department and asked the students to inculcate some of it.  

The Chief Guest, Mr Shripada Hebbar addressed the gathering wherein he said the youth of today should take care of their health, exercise regularly, get proper sleep and maintain a healthy lifestyle. He mentioned some of the key technologies that are evergreen in the industry and urged the students to master them.

Fr Wilfred Prakash D’Souza spoke about the importance of competitions and called upon the participants to compete well while playing fair. He congratulated the department of MCA for having hosted Joshiana for a decade and wished all the participants fruitful time at SJEC.

The Inaugural was compeered by Ms Diona D’Silva from 3rd year MCA.

All in all, 16 teams registered for Joshiana 10.0. The fest comprised of 10 events namely: IT Quiz, IT Manager, Coding, Web Designing, Mega Event, Mad Ads, Photography & Cinematography, Gaming, Talk Tech and Treasure Hunt.

Kristu Jayanti College, Bengaluru ended up as the Runners-Up of Joshiana 10.0, while the Overall Championship was bagged by Manipal Institute of Technology, Manipal.

MCA - TechnoCrats: Talk on Plagiarism Free Report Writing

more images

In view of Plagiarism Check by VTU for MTech/MBA/MCA Dissertation Reports, in order to inculcate the habit of respecting the academic integrity and discipline, a talk on “Plagiarism Free Report Writing” was organized by the Department of Computer Applications under the banner of TechnoCrats for the students of VI Semester MCA, IV Semester MBA as well as IV Semester MTech on Friday, 7th April 2017 from 10.00 am to 11.15 am in Fr Fred Memorial Hall of the college.

 

The resource person for the talk was Dr Rio D’Souza, Vice Principal - SJEC, who handled the session. The talk was intended to cover aspects of Anti Plagiarism regulations applicable to PG Project Thesis and Usage of Plagiarism Checking Tools. During the talk, the Vice Principal stressed the importance of plagiarism free report writing and stressed on the fact that plagiarism is not ethical. In his lecture, he explained the concept of plagiarism, types of plagiarism, 404 errors, re-tweet and the relevance of paraphrasing.

 

The Vice Principal also presented some of the tips for avoiding plagiarism and urged the students to come up with original work. He highlighted the importance of planning the report, consultation with the guide, taking effective notes, citation of the sources, and referencing. In addition to the above, he also presented the VTU guidelines for submitting the report and the permitted similarity index.

 

Apart from the MBA, MCA and MTech students, the faculties of MBA, MCA, CSE and ECE departments were also present during the talk. Overall, the session provided useful insights to the students on plagiarism free report writing.

The Technocrats - Students Association of Department of Computer Applications of SJEC inaugurated Joshiana 7.0, a 2-day National Level IT Fest for the students of MCA, MS, M.Sc., M.Tech., and BE (CS & IS). The fest is organized under the aegis of the Department of Computer Applications.

The other dignitaries present on the dais were Rev. The function began by the invocation of the Divine by Ms Helita Gras and group from III year MCA. The gathering was welcomed by Ms Sumangala N-I/C HOD MCA. Ms Prithvi V Shet, the President of The Technocrats, briefed the gathering of the events that would be held over the 2-days. This was followed by the Lighting of the Lamp which officially declared Joshiana 7.0 open and the unveiling of the trophies for which the participants were competing against each other.

The Chief Guest, Mr K Srinivas Rao addressed the gathering wherein he voiced his opinion on coping up with the growth in technology. The Principal and The Director also spoke conveying their best wishes to the participants as well as the organizers.

The curtains were drawn on the Inauguration Ceremony with the Vote of Thanks delivered by Ms Vinaya S, the Treasurer of TechnoCrats. The MC’s role for the inaugural ceremony was donned by Ms Surabhi Shetty K from III year MCA.

MCA - Technocrats: Inauguration of TechPrints 4.0

TechPrints 4.0 – A National Level Technical Paper Presentation Competition organized by the Department of Computer Applications under the banner of its Students Association - TechnoCrats, was held on Wednesday, 6th April 2016 in the Spoorthi Conference Hall, 7th Floor of Academic Block-III. TechPrints 4.0 provided a platform for budding professionals to share their ideas and discuss about the advancements in technology.

TechPrints 4.0 was inaugurated with the lighting of the lamp by the dignitaries including Mr Harish Kumar, CEO of Accolade Tech Solutions - Mangaluru, who was the Chief Guest at the Inaugural Function. The other dignitaries present on the dais were Rev Fr. Rohith D’Costa, Assistant Director, and the President of the Function, Dr Rio D’Souza, Vice Principal/Dean-MCA, Ms Sumangala N, I /C HOD MCA and Mr Gururaja S, Convener-TechPrints 4.0.  The Chief Guest, Mr Harish Kumar, addressed the gathering wherein, he voiced his opinion on coping up with the growth in technology. He also spoke about the recent trends in Android Application Development which was well received by the audience. The President of the Function, Dr Rio D’Souza appreciated the staff and students of the Department of MCA for putting up a great show. He also urged the students of the Department and the participants to take part in more such technical events wherein they get to test their technical mettle. Inaugural function also saw the release of the proceedings of TechPrints 4.0, by the Chief Guest along with the other dignitaries present during the occasion.

The conclusion of the formal function was followed by parallel presentations which were held at 2 different venues simultaneously. Papers were called for the topics under the broad categories of Data Mining/Neural Networks/Machine Learning/Image Processing/Pattern Recognition & Big Data on one side and Computer Networks/Mobile Computing/Cloud Computing/Multimedia Applications on the other side. All in all, 30 papers were shortlisted and were called for presentation on the occasion. Out of the 30 presentations, 12 were under Stream 1 and 18 were under Stream 2. The presentations under Stream 1 are being judged by Dr Reeja S R, Associate Professor-CSE and Mr Raghavendra Achar, Assistant Professor-CSE whereas the presentations under Stream 2 are being judged by Mr Shreenath Acharya, Assistant Professor-CSE and Mr Sunil Kamath, Assistant Professor-CSE.

MCA - TechnoCrats: Talk on Plagiarism Free Report Writing

more images

In view of the introduction of Plagiarism Check by VTU for M.Tech/MBA/MCA Dissertations, in order to inculcate the habit of respecting the academic integrity and discipline, a talk on Plagiarism Free Report Writing was organized by the Department of Computer Applications under the banner of TechnoCrats for the students of VI Semester MCA, IV Semester MBA as well as IV Semester M.Tech on Monday, 4th April 2016 from 11.00 am to 12.00 noon in Fr Fred Memorial Hall of the college.

The resource person for the talk was Dr Rio D’Souza, Vice Principal - SJEC, who handled the session. The talk was intended to cover aspects of Anti Plagiarism regulations applicable to PG Project Thesis and Usage of Plagiarism Checking Tools. During the talk, Dr Rio D’Souza stressed the importance of plagiarism free report writing and stressed on the fact that plagiarism is not ethical. In his lecture, he explained the concept of plagiarism, types of plagiarism, 404 errors, re-tweet and the relevance of paraphrasing.

The Vice Principal also presented some tips for avoiding plagiarism and urged the students to come up with original work. He highlighted the importance of planning the report, consultation with the guide, taking effective notes, citation of the sources, and referencing. In addition to the above, he also presented the VTU guidelines for submitting the report and the permitted similarity index.

Apart from the MBA, MCA and M.Tech students, the faculties of MCA and MBA Departments were also present during the talk. Overall, the session provided useful insights to the students on plagiarism free report writing.

Inauguration of Yuvalakshya 2016

more images

Yuvalakshya 2016 - a UG Level Fest is being organized by the Departments of Business Administration (MBA) and Computer Applications (MCA) of St Joseph Engineering College, Mangaluru. The fest is being organized for the students of BCA, BSc, BCom and BBM students from in and around Karnataka.

Yuvalakshya 2016 was inaugurated on Wednesday, 10th February 2016 with the lighting the lamp by the dignitaries which included Mr Brian Fernandes, Chief Officer-Operations, Spearhead Media [P] Ltd, who was the Chief Guest at the Inaugural Function. The other dignitaries present on the dais were Rev Fr Joseph Lobo, Director-SJEC who presided over the ceremony, Rev Fr Rohith D’Costa, Assistant Director-SJEC, Dr Joseph Gonsalvis, Principal-SJEC, Dr Rio D’Souza, Vice Principal/Dean-MCA-SJEC, Dr Prakash Pinto, Dean MBA/Convener Yuvalakshya and Ms Anushree Raj, HOD MCA/Co-Convener Yuvalakshya.

The function began by the invocation of the Divine. The gathering was welcomed by Ms Varsha Baliga - I MBA, who extended warm wishes to all the people present during the Inaugural and dignitaries were then felicitated by the students. This was followed by the Lighting of the Lamp which officially declared Yuvalakshya 2016 open.

The Chief Guest, Mr Brian Fernandes addressed the gathering wherein he stressed the importance of excelling in the field of an individual’s choice. He mentioned that every individual is unique and special in their own right. He also urged the participants to compete with themselves in order to get the best out of them. At the end, he wished that Yuvalakshya would become a grand success and appreciated the organizers for organizing an event of this stature. This was followed by the addressing of the gathering by the Principal and Director who motivated the participants with their words of wisdom.

The newsletters of MBA Department - Yuktha and MCA Department - Tantrakosha, were released by the dignitaries during the occasion.

The curtains were drawn on the Inauguration Ceremony with the Vote of Thanks delivered by Mr Johnson Mascarenhas, I MBA, thereby declaring the fest open to all. The MC’s role for the inaugural ceremony was donned by Ms Diana Mathias from I year MBA. The Inaugural programme was followed by a video presentation which introduced SJEC to the participants from various UG Colleges.

The events are being conducted by the students of MBA and MCA with the judges being invited from in and out of the college. All in all 25 teams have registered for Yuvalakshya 2016 under BCom and BBM category and 27 teams under the category of BCA and BSc. Around 500 participants are being a part of the fest.

The fest is comprised of 9 events namely - Ashwamedha (Business Quiz), Padmavyuha (Mad Ad), Akriti (Painting) and Koshadhyaksh (Treasure Hunt) which are being conducted by the department of MBA for the students of BCom and BBM along with IT Quiz, IT Collage, IT Product Launch, IT Dumb Charades and Gaming which are being conducted by the department of MCA for the students of BCA and BSc. The events are designed to test the management and technical mettle of the participants.
