About
The Central Library

K.V.G College of Engineering Library was started at Polytechnic building under the name of KVG. Initially set up in 1986, the Central Library moved to Engineering College in 1990.The KVGCE library consists of a Central Library and six departmental libraries which collectively support the teaching, Research and extension programmes of the institute. The library provides a wide range of books which include university prescribed text books and reference materials on engineering, science and humanities offers library services through its various Divisions. It’s collection of about 60933 volumes and 27500 Titles, above 6000 print/online scholarly journals are used by more than 1200 members and others. Library is also an active member of VTU Consortium, DELNET and National Digital Library.

Library Vision

“Library aims to be best among engineering college libraries in the country. Library intends to incorporate the latest technology and adopt user friendly approach towards students and faculty. Library intends to offer comprehensive services related to dissemination of knowledge.”

Library Mission

“The Library's mission is to provide comprehensive resources and services in support of the research, teaching, and learning needs of the faculty and students of the institute and provides an efficient dissemination of knowledge.”

Objectives

To provide the right information at the right time in the right form.

To facilitate creation of new knowledge.

To optimize the use of information resources by inculcating reading habits among the users.

To offer a fully integrated and dynamic environment suited for academic pursuits, study and research.

To promote awareness of library and information resources through user awareness programmes.

ONE WEEK ONLINE FDP ON "INSIGHTS ON WRITING RESEARCH PROPOSALS & FUNDING OPPORTUNITIES"

more images

The Department Of Central Library In Association With Research And Facilities Group organized ONE WEEK ONLINE FDP ON "INSIGHTS ON WRITING RESEARCH PROPOSALS & FUNDING OPPORTUNITIES".

As many educational institutions move to a remote learning environment, a lot of researchers around the world are experiencing online mode in every stage. To promote the same, the Research and Facilities Group of St Joseph Engineering College (SJEC), Mangaluru, organized One Week Online FDP on Insights on Writing Research Proposals and Funding Opportunities to Research Scholars, Faculty and Students from 20 to 24 July 2020 via Cisco WebEx.

The objectives of the event were to provide knowledge on Research Proposal and Funding Opportunities for academicians as well as scientists working on research. The FDP was also intended at providing insights on promoting excellence by knowing ways to get DST, CSIR, DRDO, MHRD funds and to create awareness to write good proposals. To provide inputs on these, a group of well-experienced experts were identified who shared their valuable knowledge and experience during the week-long event.

The FDP was virtually inaugurated by the Chief Guest Dr Venkateswarlu Karodi, Senior Principal Scientist, CSIR, NAL, Bengaluru, in the presence of Rev. Fr Wilfred Prakash D'Souza, Director of SJEC, Rev. Fr Rohith D'Costa and Rev. Fr. Alwyn Richard D'Souza, Assistant Directors of SJEC, Dr Rio D'Souza, Principal of SJEC, Dr Felcy D'Souza, Librarian of SJEC and Convener of the FDP and Dr Sandhya Dass, Chairperson of Research and Facilities Group at SJEC and Chief Coordinator of the FDP.

Dr Venkateswarlu Karodi, Chief Guest of the FDP, in his keynote address stated that organizing such events would be an effort to reach out and guide the prospective research scholars to learn something which helps them to bring out quality publications.

After the keynote, the following sessions were conducted over five days:

1. Writing a Research Proposal by Dr Rajalaxmi Chouhan, Assistant Professor, Dept of Electrical Engineering, IIT Jodhpur and Recipient of 'Early Career Research Award'

2. How to write Acceptable Research Papers and Role of Research Supervisors by Dr Arun Mohan Isloor, Professor and Head, Department of Chemistry, NITK, Surathkal

3. Research Support Tools & Techniques by Dr Felcy D'Souza, Librarian, SJEC

4. Funding Opportunities to Start-ups by Dr Y S Upadhyay, Associate Professor (Senior Scale), Department of Mechanical & Manufacturing Engineering, MIT, Manipal

5. Funding Agencies for Independent Researchers by Dr Mahedevappa Manjunath, Associate Professor, School of Medical Science & Technology, IIT Kharagpur and Vice-Chairman of GATE-JAM

6. Biotechnology-Research and Entrepreneurship Opportunities by Dr Prashantha Naik, Professor, Dept of Biosciences, Mangalore University

7. Research and Higher Education by Dr Rajesh Kumar P C, Associate Professor, Dept of Physics, SJEC

8. Secrets to Write Publishable High Impact Research Articles and Winning Grant Proposals by Dr B Sathiyabhama, Professor and Head, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Sona College of Technology, Salem

9. Writing Research Proposals by Dr Abhishek Appaji, Institutional Coordinator for R&D, Jt. Treasurer & SIGHT Chair, IEEE Bengaluru Chapter, BMS. College of Engineering, Bengaluru

The well-attended program had more than 250 participants from SJEC including invited delegates ranging from academicians, professionals from Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Orissa, Telangana etc. The program was also marked by lively talks, questions, and interactions with the participants

8. WORKSHOP ON SCOPUS & MENDELEY: TOOLS FOR EMPOWERING RESEARCH

more images

The Library Advisory Committee in association with Department of Computer Applications organized a one day workshop on "Scopus & Mendeley: Tools for Empowering Research" on 21st September 2019 at Bishop Aloysius Paul Conference Hall.

Prof Shivaram B S, Information Scientist, Council for Scientific and Industrial Research-National Aerospace Laboratory (CSIR-NAL), Bengaluru was the Resource Person of the workshop.Dr Felcy D'Souza, Librarian introduced the resource person and highlighted the objectives of the workshop i.e., to empower the research scholars and faculty members to keep track of the current developments in their research/interest and to organize their references during their research study or writing papers for publications in the journals/conferences/seminars.

There were three sessions namely, Publication Metrics for Researchers, Information Discovery: Tips, Tools & Techniques and hands on training session on Reference Management Tool-Mendeley by Prof Shivaram B S.

The topics covered in the first session are: Importance of publication metrics for researchers & NIRF ranking of the Institute, sources of publications metrics, citation analysis, Journal Impact Factor, author metrics, H-index calculation, analysis of citation profile, author profile and publication profile of the Institute by SCOPUS.

In the second session the topics covered were as follows; Journal selection tools for publishing research work, research language, author name ambiguity, copy right & its infringement etc.

The Third session was on hands on experience on Reference Management Tool: Mendeley.

The above sessions concluded with an open discussion and feedback from participants. At the end as a token of love and appreciation the Resource Person was presented with a memento by Ms Sumangala N, Chairperson, Library & Learning Committee. Ms Soumya Asst Prof and Coordinator of the workshop proposed the vote of thanks. The programme was compered by Ms Avila Pinto, Asst Prof, E&C Department.

A total of 65 research scholars and faculty members from SJEC and external research scholars pursuing research under SJEC Research Centre participated in the workshop.

NATIONAL WORKSHOP ON DELNET: RESOURCES, SERVICES AND FACILITIES AND KOHA

more images

Central Library SJEC in collaboration with DELNET New Delhi jointly organized one day National workshop on "DELNET: Resources, Services and Facilities and KOHA: an open source library integrated management software on 6th May 2014. More than 80 Library professionals, LIS students from Mangalore University and Research Scholars from various Institutions, in and around Karnataka were participated in the workshop.

Dr. Khaiser Muneebulla Khan, Professor and Chairman of the Department of Library and Information Science in Mangalore University, was the Chief Guest of the inaugural function. Dr. Felcy D'Souza Librarian SJEC and the co-ordinator of the workshop welcomed the gathering. Key note address was delivered by Dr. H.K.Kaul, Director, DELNET, New Delhi. The objectives of the workshop were highlighted by Dr. Sangeetha Kaul, Network Manager, DELENT. Presidential address was delivered by Rev. Fr. Joseph J Lobo, Director- SJEC.

The workshop comprised of three sessions namely  "New Trends in Managing Libraries", presented by Dr. H K Kaul, followed by "Networking Libraries through DELNET" by Dr. Sangeeta Kaul, and Omkar N Safapuri, Consultant - DELNET Coordination Unit, Bangalore, and "Consortium for E-journals and E-books". The post lunch-break sessions consisted of "Koha: an Open Source Integrated Library Management System, by Dr Sangeeta Kaul. The sessions concluded with an open discussion and feedback from participants of member- institutions. At the end as a token of love and appreciation the dignitaries were presented with a memento by Dr. Joseph Gonsalves, Principal-SJEC. Dr. Prakash Pinto, Dean MBA and the Chairperson of the Library Committee proposed the vote of thanks. Ms. Olivia Sequiera, Asst. Prof. Physics Department was the MC of the program.

REPORT ON USER AWARENESS WORKSHOP ON IEEE E-RESOURCE”

St. Joseph Engineering College IEEE Student Branch in association with Central Library organized user awareness workshop on “IEEE Explore Digital Library” on 7 th April 2017. The Workshop began with a prayer song led by College Choir. Dr. Nalini Rebello, HOD, Civil Engineering and Chairperson of the Library Advisory Committee welcomed the gathering. Dr. Felcy D’Souza, Librarian, introduced the resource person Mr. Akshay Prasanna, Sr. Manager – Global Information Systems and Technologies Pvt. Ltd (GIST), Bangalore. The main objective of the workshop was to bring the awareness about IEEE and its resources among faculty, research scholars and students which would help in their research and publications and also to enhance the knowledge.

The Resource Person gave a detailed insight on IEEE and its resources. He also briefed about the basic search, advanced search, remote access, up-to- date access through mobile apps, choosing topics for projects from IEEE and its importance for placements, creating alerts on new articles and topics, different type of search techniques which help to narrow down the search results etc. He also added on how to publish paper in IEEE conferences and journals and its importance to the students to strengthen their profile for placements as well as for seeking higher studies in abroad. He also highlighted on the free courses available on subjects and plagiarism. There was a good response from the faculty, research scholars and students, who have actively participated in the interactive session and cleared their doubts. The session was informative and very useful. At the end, Dr. Bini A.A, Associate Professor and member of Library Advisory Committee proposed the vote of thanks. Mr ArunAlvares IEEE SJEC Student Branch Chair honoured the resource person and Ms Alisha Patrao compered the program. Around 35 faculty members/research scholars and 20 students participated in the workshop.
