About
Electronics & Communications Department

The Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering was setup during the inception of the college in 2002. With the effort of well qualified faculty and best infrastructure, the department has grown from strength to strength in the last decade. Currently, the student intake is 120 at the UG level and 18 at the PG level. Also, the VTU has approved the department to offer Ph.D / M.Sc (Engg) by research from 2015. The faculty of the department are actively involved in teaching and research with specializations in Communication, Signal Processing, Embedded System and VLSI. The department encourage students to present papers in symposium and conferences, and to participate in various intercollege technical and nontechnical events.

The department is well equipped with state-of-art laboratories with the latest tools such as Cadence VLSI Design Suite, Xilinx Vivado-2018, MATLAB 2018, ARM CORTEX, LABView, Multisim and NEXYS 4 DDR Artix-7 FPGA Board, to make students Industry ready. In addition, the department has Biomedical Electronics Research Lab to encourage students to develop projects in the field of biomedical signal processing in association with Father Muller’s Research Centre and Hospital.

The department has MOU with “Karmic Design Pvt Ltd” Manipal to facilitate the students in semiconductor chip design. The department actively organizes Industry visits, National Level Conference, Workshops, Technical talks from Industry/Academic experts in order to enhance their learning abilities, creative thinking and also to bridge the gap between the Industry and Academia.

The Department of Electronics and Communication is proud to announce that the UG Student project titled “Automated Union Over Touch Enabled System(AUTES) “has been awarded as the Best Project of the Year 2017-18 (41 st Series), by KSCST Government of Karnataka.

Vision

"To Excel in Electronics and Communication Engineering Education and Research, focusing on the needs of Industry and Society, with professional ethics"

Mission

  • Provide opportunities to deserving students for quality professional education in the field of Electronics and Communication.
  • Design and deliver curricula to meet the changing needs of industry through student centric learning methodologies to excel in their profession.
  • Recruit, Nurture and Retain best faculty and technical manpower.
  • Consolidate the state-of-art infrastructure and equipment for teaching and research activities.
  • Promote all round personality development of the students through interaction with alumni, academia and industry.
  • Strengthen the Educational Social Responsibilities of the institution.

Programme Educational Objectives (PEOs)

  • To provide students with the solid foundation in mathematical, scientific, Electronics and Communication engineering to analyze data and technical concepts for application to product design and also to pursue higher education.
  • To train students with good scientific and engineering breadth, including proficiency in software language and use of latest software tools so as to comprehend, analyze, design and create novel products and solutions for the real life problems.
  • To develop skills in students for successful careers in industry that meet the needs of Indian and multinational companies, through rigorous education
  • To inculcate in students professional and ethical attitude, effective communication skills and teamwork, multidisciplinary approach, and an ability to relate engineering issues to broader social context.
  • To provide students with an academic environment to become aware of excellence, leadership, written ethical codes and guidelines, and the life-long learning needed for a successful professional career.

Programme Outcomes (POs)

    Engineering Graduate shall be able to:
  • Apply knowledge of mathematics, science and engineering fundamentals, and Electronics and Communication Engineering for the solution of engineering problems.
  • Identify, formulate and solve engineering problems.
  • Design electronic systems, components or processes to meet desired specifications within realistic constraints of economic and environmental standards.
  • Design and conduct experiments, as well as to analyze and interpret data pertaining to electronic systems.
  • Use computer aided software tools and techniques for solving electronics and communication engineering problems.
  • Demonstrate awareness of contemporary engineering problems.
  • Apply engineering solutions in societal and environmental context.
  • Understand professional and ethical responsibility.
  • Function within multidisciplinary teams.
  • Communicate effectively in terms of system specifications within the team.
  • Demonstrate the understanding of management principles as applied to the specified work and apply this knowledge to manage the projects as a member and leader in a team.
  • Continue the education in self-learning mode.

Programme Specific Outcomes(PSOs)

    Engineering Graduate shall be able to:
  • Participate and succeed in competitive examinations.
  • Understand technological advances in industry through Industry Interaction.

The Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering, SJEC organized a FDP on"Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning" on 24th  February 2022 in Prerana Hall, Ground floor, Academic Block III. ECE: A Technical Talk on “Recent Trends in Advanced Industries”

more images

Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering organized a technical talk on "Recent Trends in Advanced Industries" to the 3rd and 4th year students by the resource person Mr Himanshu Kumar on 12th December 2021.

The Program started at 2:00 p.m. in the Fr. Fred Hall. The chief guest, Mr. Himanshu Kumar was cordially welcomed by Joel Mascarenhas along with the introduction of resource person. Faculties Ms Padmini Bhat, Dr Phalguna P.S and Mr Glenson Toney were also present throughout the programme. The talk was based on role of Industrial Automation in manufacturing industries and hands on session of PLC programming.

The session started with introduction of Industrial automation. Five layers of automation systems were introduced to the students. The session included Field level, Control level, Supervisory level, Planning level and Management level. He also introduced some of the basics sensors or development boards used within these levels. Later session continued with basics of PLC programming. The students were thought of PLC programming and also given some tasks for the same. He also briefed about the software such as SCADA, HMI, DRIVE etc. Lastly, the Hardware interface of PLC with sensor, switch, motor, indicator, and SCADA software was shown in real time.

The session was really informative. The students were able to analyse the basics of PLC programming, Importance of Automation required in industries, recent industrial technology trends and various software's used to control PLC. Overall, it was very useful session and good feedback was received from the audience. Finally, the event concluded with the valedictory program. Mr Joel Mascarenhas proposed the vote of thanks.

Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering of SJEC organized a three-day workshop on “Communication System and Signal Processing using MATLAB” from 29th January 2019 to 31st January for pre final year students. The workshop was attended by 18 students from Department of Electronics and Communication. The workshop was scheduled from 9.00 a.m. to 5.00 p.m. Three sessions were conducted   on each day.  The resource person   were,   Dr Savitha H M, Ms Nandini Maninarayana and Ms Rashmi H.

Firstly, the MATLAB basics were revised. The session continued with an introduction to the communication system, along with a brief discussion on Analog communication, which included Amplitude modulation and frequency modulation. Adding on more to the session, Digital Communication Scheme that covered M-ASK, M-PSK, M-QAM and Constellation diagram with relevant MATLAB codes was discussed. An insight on B-PSK and the associated code was also given. Simulink had been introduced at the end of the day. Simulation of the full adder, realizing up counter using JK flip-flop and the use of HDL coder was implemented on Simulink.

The second day began with the continuation of the session on Simulink for Amplitude Modulation and Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK). Under BPSK, Bit Error Rate v/s Signal to Noise Ratio had been determined. A demonstration of Constellation diagram was also done for BPSK, QPSK and 8 – PSK. Audio processing was also done using Simulink. Following the constellation diagram, filter design application and Image processing had been introduced to the students. Under Image processing, reading an image, conversion of an image to grayscale and to black and white, extraction of R, G and B components, thresholding, resizing, cropping and rotating the image had been explained with the related codes.

The third day, commenced with edge detection of an image i.e, Sobel, Canny and Prewitt edge detection, followed by RGB concatenation and color segmentation programs. Later that day, the session dealt with the use of app designer, hardware interface (interfaced Arduino with MATLAB to blink and control the brightness of the LED) and an introduction to machine learning app. The workshop concluded on the third day with an introduction to the IoT platform, Thingspeak.
