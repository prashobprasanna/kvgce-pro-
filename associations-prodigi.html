ECE: A Session on "Writing A Literature Review"

more images

The Department of Electronics & Communication Engg of SJEC presented a talk on Writing A Literature Review as an activity of PRODGI- Student's Association & Industry Oriented Activities Lab & Mini project lab on Saturday, December 04, 2021.

The main objective of the talk was to address the students of 5th Sem ECE regarding the literature survey and review aspect of their upcoming mini project, which is an important section of their Phase 1 internal evaluation.

The talk was presented to both A and B sections of 5th Sem ECE in two separate classrooms. The resource persons were Dr Dayakshini , Dr Phalguna P S, and Mr Glenson Tony.

The talk began at 2 pm with a brief introduction of the matter by Dr Dayakshini, HOD of ECE Department followed by welcoming the gathering by Ms Jennifer Charlotte Saldanha, Asst Professor, Dept of ECE.

The key points discussed and key takeaways from the talk were:

·         The need for literature review in project report

·         Difference between literature survey and literature review

·         Way of reading a literature paper

·         Renowned publishers of journals and conferences

·         Identification of trusted sources

·         Procedures to follow while filling the literature review canvas

·         Various referencing styles used

The program concluded with rendering vote of thanks by Mr Glenson Tony.

Workshop on "Research Paper/Report Writing, Plagiarism and Ethical Issues"

more images

PRODIGI-ECE student association organized a two day workshop on "Research Paper/Report Writing, Plagiarism and Ethical Issues" on talk on 23 and 24 March, 2021 at Fr Fred Memorial Hall. The workshop was focused on giving insights on the aspects such as: how to write a project report, how to write a research paper, awareness on how to avoid plagiarism in writing report, ethical issues in research and documentation, Tools for writing project report, project presentations and research paper such as MS word, PowerPoint Presentation. Workshop also included hands-on session on LaTeX tool for report/paper writing.

First session of the day one, was on the topic "How to write a research paper and project report" which was conducted by Dr Rohan Pinto, Associate Professor, ECE dept. In this session, the resource person gave useful tips on how to plan and organize project report, what should be the content for different sections of the project report. How to write a research article in an effective manner so that it gets accepted by the conference/journals.

Second session of the day one, was on the topic "Plagiarism and Ethical issues" which was conducted by Ms Jennifer C Saldanha, Assistant Professor , ECE Dept. The session was mainly focused on giving awareness to the students on "Plagiarism". In her talk she explained different ways to avoid plagiarism in the documentation. She also gave useful information on how to paraphrase the text and different types of citation styles to avoid plagiarism. The talk gave insights on ethical issues in research, wherein she mentioned about different types of research misconduct, role of ethical approval committee for bio medical research and conducted case studies for the students to identify unethical behaviours in research.

Third session of the day one, was on MS word, Power Point Presentation Tools for research documentation which was delivered by Mr Keith R Fernandes, Assistant Professor, ECE Dept. In this session, the resource person demonstrated different options available in MS word which will be useful for students while writing the project report. He also demonstrated mendeley tool for arranging the references. The Day 1 ended with resource person demonstrating power point presentation tool for preparing the project presentations.

The first session of the day two, was on LaTeX tool for documentation which was conducted by Ms Dayakshini, HOD-ECE. The session stared with the brief introduction of the tool and how it is useful for research documentation.  The introduction was followed by hands on session wherein the resource person demonstrated different examples to introduce participants to the LaTeX tool and its usefulness in the documentation to write report and thesis.

The last session of the workshop was on TURNITIN software for plagiarism check, handled by Ms Shama B N, Assistant Professor, ECE Dept. She demonstrated the steps to be followed while using the software by staff and students. She also explained typical plagiarism report generated by TURNITIN software and how to analyze the report.

The workshop was organized and moderated by Ms Vijayalaxmi H M and Ms Jennifer C Saldanha, Co-ordinators, PRODIGI 2020-21.

Talk on Research on Control Engineering

more images

PRODIGI-ECE student association organized talk on Research on Control Engineering to the students of ECE and EEE departments on 7-11-2020. This lecture was mainly focused on the modelling of nonlinear systems and to design linear and nonlinear control systems from the basics to advanced structures. The seminar started at 11.30 am by moderator Ms Loshika Lokesh, 5th ECE student, Dr. Dayakshini HOD, ECE Department welcomed the attendees and introduced the speaker to the participants.

 

Overview and topics of discussion: Engineering systems are nonlinear in nature. Based on needs and convenience, engineers linearize the systems for modeling, control and implementation to improve the closed loop performance with and without disturbance. To explore the challenges and to effectively apply the engineering mathematics, one should deal with the system's nonlinearity. Taylor series plays a major role to deal with the nonlinear systems. This entire webinar was focused on the modeling of nonlinear systems and to design the class of linear and nonlinear control system from the basic to advanced structures.

 

The talk was followed by question answer session monitored by Ms Jennifer C Saldhana. The session ended with the vote of thanks by Ms Vijayalaxmi H M. Total 305 participants attended the session. At the end of the session the feedback link was shared with the participants. The webinar is well appreciated by the participants.

ECE: ARDUINO WORKSHOP

more images

Arduino workshop was conducted in the Department of Electronics and Communication on 25th March 2017 (Saturday). Workshop on Arduino Basic-V1 was conducted by Ms. Lavanya B L, Assistant Professor, E&C Department under Prodigi and Pioneer student association.

This workshop provides a platform for students to understand fundamentals of Arduino and to understand the basic procedures for working with Arduino board. The Arduino is a cheap, flexible, open source microcontroller platform designed to make it easy for hobbyists to use electronics in homemade projects. It runs on Mac, Windows, and Linux. The Arduino Uno is a microcontroller board based on the ATmega328 (datasheet). It has 14 digital input/output pins (of which 6 can be used as PWM outputs), 6 analog inputs, a 16 MHz ceramic resonator, a USB connection, a power jack, an ICSP header, and a reset button.

The duration of this workshop will be one and half days (or 3 afternoon session) with three and a half hours session on each half day, in a total of ten and a half hours divided into theory and hands on sessions. At the end of this workshop a competition (exhibition of their own project) will be organized among the participating students and each participating student will get certificate of participation.

Total registration for the workshop is 123 students out of which 69 second year students, 51 third year students and 3 final year students. The first 38 registered students workshop was conducted on 25th March 2017 (Saturday) from 1.30 P.M. to 5.00 P.M. in LD lab Room No (1210 A) and AE Lab Room No  (1210 C ) with the help of 4 student volunteers, Mr. Vijay Ganesh P, Pioneer co-ordinator and Mr.  Prasad S M, Prodigi co-ordinator.

Contents covered in the workshop: Introduction to Arduino and its Features, Installing the Software Arduino IDE, Basic of arduino coding, programing the Serial Monitor, Different LED programs (ON the LED, Blink the LED, Pushbutton to on and off the LED, Traffic Light, RGB LED, User input to on and off LED-Serial Monitor, Bluetooth controlled LED), Relay to on off the Bulb using bluetooth and Servo Motor Control.

ECE: Talk on “Advances in VLSI Circuits”

more images

PRODIGI – the Department Association of Electronics & Communication Engineering organized a technical talk on Advances in VLSI Circuits by Mr Rohan Pinto, Assistant Professor, E&C, on 3 September 2014, from 11:30 am to 1:00 pm, in Fr Fred Memorial Hall, Academic Block I. The talk was attended by final year BE and M Tech students of E&C.

The speaker began with an introduction to Integrated Circuits (IC) and its evolution starting from the early 1960’s till date. He briefly explained the different generations of IC design and why the MOS transistor is a fundamental component of any IC processor. He introduced the students to different techniques that are followed in companies like Intel, TSMC, Apple, and Samsung, to reduce the size of MOS transistors that in turn reduces the size of the processor enabling them to be faster and yet consume less power.

He spoke about the rapid growth in the IC industry and the fact that semiconductor manufacturers are working with 22nm technology while research is being carried out to design transistors with 7nm technology.

He also explained details about different materials such as: High K Dialectics and Metal Gates that are used to make present day MOS transistors and how this affects the performance of a device as the transistor size shrinks. He concluded with the development of the smart phone industry that has given rise to an entirely new range of processors which offer high performance, portability and less consumption of power.

The Q&A session that followed the talk was very productive. Dr Savitha H M, Professor, E&C, presented Mr Rohan Pinto with a certificate of appreciation. Ms Panchmi, VII E&C, was the emcee and proposed the Vote of Thanks.
